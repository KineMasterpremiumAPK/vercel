Best Video Editing Apps for Mac and Windows in 2025

Video editing has become more accessible than ever, thanks to powerful software available for both Mac and Windows. Whether you're a professional editor or a beginner looking to create stunning videos, the right editing app can make a significant difference. In this guide, we explore the best video editing apps for Mac and Windows in 2025, highlighting their features, pros, and cons to help you find the perfect fit.

Factors to Consider When Choosing a Video Editing App

Before diving into the best video editing software, consider these key factors:

  • Ease of Use – Is the software beginner-friendly or designed for professionals?
  • Editing Features – Does it include essential tools like transitions, color correction, and multi-layer editing?
  • Performance & Speed – Can it handle high-resolution files efficiently?
  • Export Options – Are there multiple format options for different platforms?
  • Price – Is it free, subscription-based, or a one-time purchase?

Best Video Editing Apps for Mac and Windows in 2025

1. Adobe Premiere Pro

Platform: Mac & Windows
Best for: Professionals and advanced users
Price: Subscription-based ($20.99/month)

Adobe Premiere Pro remains a top choice for professional video editors. With advanced features like multi-camera editing, motion graphics, and seamless integration with Adobe Creative Cloud, it’s a powerhouse for creating high-quality content.

  • Pros: Industry-standard editing tools, AI-powered auto-reframe and speech-to-text, seamless integration with After Effects & Photoshop.
  • Cons: Steeper learning curve, subscription cost can be high.

2. Final Cut Pro

Platform: Mac
Best for: Professional Mac users
Price: One-time purchase ($299.99)

Apple’s Final Cut Pro is a fantastic choice for Mac users, offering a smooth workflow, magnetic timeline, and optimized performance for Apple’s M1 & M2 chips. It provides advanced color grading, HDR support, and professional-level audio editing.

  • Pros: Optimized for Mac hardware, one-time purchase (no subscription), high-speed rendering and export.
  • Cons: Not available for Windows users, learning curve for beginners.

3. DaVinci Resolve

Platform: Mac & Windows
Best for: Advanced users and color grading professionals
Price: Free version available; DaVinci Resolve Studio ($295 one-time purchase)

DaVinci Resolve is known for its powerful color correction tools and advanced editing capabilities. The free version offers extensive features, while the Studio version unlocks even more professional tools.

  • Pros: Best-in-class color grading, free version with pro-level features, Fusion for visual effects and motion graphics.
  • Cons: Can be resource-intensive, steeper learning curve.

4. HitFilm Express

Platform: Mac & Windows
Best for: Beginners and aspiring filmmakers
Price: Free (Pro version available)

HitFilm Express is an excellent free video editing app packed with VFX tools and essential editing features. It’s great for content creators who need high-quality visual effects without breaking the bank.

  • Pros: Free with optional paid upgrades, VFX tools for special effects, beginner-friendly interface.
  • Cons: Some features locked behind paywalls, requires a decent PC/Mac to run smoothly.

5. Filmora

Platform: Mac & Windows
Best for: Beginners and intermediate users
Price: Subscription-based ($49.99/year) or lifetime license ($79.99)

Filmora is a user-friendly video editing app that caters to beginners and intermediate users. It includes drag-and-drop editing, built-in effects, and AI-powered tools for quick video creation.

  • Pros: Intuitive and beginner-friendly, affordable pricing options, AI-powered editing tools.
  • Cons: Lacks advanced features for professionals, subscription model may not appeal to all.

6. CyberLink PowerDirector

Platform: Mac & Windows
Best for: Content creators and YouTubers
Price: Subscription-based ($4.33/month) or lifetime purchase

CyberLink PowerDirector is a great choice for YouTubers and casual editors. It provides fast rendering, AI-powered tools, and a vast library of effects and transitions.

  • Pros: Affordable and feature-packed, AI-powered editing features, fast rendering speeds.
  • Cons: Some advanced features require a paid version, UI can feel cluttered for beginners.

Conclusion

Choosing the best video editing app depends on your needs, budget, and skill level. If you’re a professional editor, Adobe Premiere Pro or Final Cut Pro might be the best choice. If you're on a budget but need advanced features, DaVinci Resolve is a fantastic option. Meanwhile, beginners will find Filmora and HitFilm Express easy to learn and effective.
